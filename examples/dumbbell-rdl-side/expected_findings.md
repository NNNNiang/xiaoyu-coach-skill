# Expected Findings: Dumbbell RDL Side

## 中文验收要点

这份示例用于测试小鱼教练对髋铰链动作的评估能力。生成报告时不需要逐字复制本文件，但应保留下面这些判断。

### 本示例测试什么

- 髋铰链动作复盘。
- 底部行程和腰背位置的安全判断。
- 区分罗马尼亚硬拉 / 微屈膝直腿硬拉与深蹲模式。
- 侧面机位的拍摄建议。

### 必须观察到

- 说明动作是否由髋部后移主导。
- 说明膝盖是否保持微屈，而不是锁死。
- 评价底部时背部是否保持延展。
- 评价哑铃是否贴近腿部移动。
- 评价是否为了追求更低位置而牺牲腰背位置。

### 安全优先级期望

- 报告必须包含安全优先级。
- 腰背位置和底部控制是主要安全重点。
- 如果底部出现圆背或脊柱位置明显变化，应建议先缩短行程，再考虑增加重量。
- 不做医学诊断，用训练风险语言表达。

### 不应误判

- 不要因为膝盖有轻微弯曲就把它说成深蹲。
- 不要要求膝盖完全锁死；髋铰链动作中微屈膝是合理的。
- 不要从纯侧面角度强行判断左右重心偏移。
- 不要暴露姿态引擎、抽帧、模型识别等内部术语。

### 拍摄建议期望

- 保留侧面机位，用于判断髋铰链、腰背位置和哑铃路径。
- 如果重点是左右偏移、脚掌压力或双侧哑铃路径，再增加斜前方机位。

## English Baseline

Use this file as a regression target when testing Xiaoyu Coach on this sample. The exact wording of a generated report may differ, but the report should preserve these coaching judgments.

## What This Sample Tests

- Hip-hinge movement review.
- Bottom-range safety and back-position coaching.
- Distinguishing a Romanian deadlift / soft-knee straight-leg deadlift from a squat pattern.
- Side-angle filming advice.

## Required Observations

- Mention whether the movement is led by the hips moving back.
- Mention whether the knees stay softly bent rather than locked.
- Review whether the back stays long through the bottom range.
- Comment on dumbbell path, especially whether the dumbbells stay close to the legs.
- Comment on whether the lifter forces extra depth at the expense of back position.

## Safety Priority Expectations

- The report should include a safety-priority section.
- Back-position and bottom-range control should be the main safety focus.
- If the bottom position looks like it risks lumbar rounding or excessive spinal movement, the report should recommend reducing range before increasing load.
- The report should avoid medical diagnosis; use training-risk language instead.

## Do Not Misjudge

- Do not call this a squat just because the knees flex slightly.
- Do not require locked knees; soft knees are appropriate for this hinge pattern.
- Do not judge left-right weight shift strongly from a pure side angle.
- Do not expose internal pose-engine or frame-selection terminology.

## Expected Filming Advice

- Keep the side angle for hip hinge, back position, and dumbbell path.
- Add a front-oblique angle only when left-right shift, foot pressure, or uneven dumbbell path is the main question.
