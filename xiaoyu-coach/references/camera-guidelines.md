# Camera Guidelines

Use camera guidance when evaluating video quality or writing filming advice.

## General Rules

- Film one full working set, not only selected reps.
- Keep the whole body, implement, and machine path in frame.
- Use stable framing; avoid zooming during the set.
- Put the camera around waist-to-chest height unless the movement needs a special view.
- If the user labels the camera angle incorrectly, trust the visible video over the label.

## Preferred Angles

| Movement | Primary angle | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Squat / hack squat | side or rear-side 30-45 degrees | depth, torso angle, hip-knee timing, bar or sled path |
| Deadlift / RDL | side 30-45 degrees | hinge, back line, bar path, knee drift |
| Bench press | side-front 30-45 degrees | bar path, touch point, elbow line, foot stability |
| Dumbbell press | side-front | shoulder position, depth, left-right sync |
| Pulldown / pull-up | front or rear-front | shoulder depression, elbow path, torso lean |
| Row | side-front 30-45 degrees | handle path, torso stability, elbow route |
| Lateral raise / fly / face pull | front or slight front-side | symmetry, top range, shrugging |
| Leg extension / leg curl | side-front | knee range, top/bottom control, body bracing |

## Report Wording

Keep advice practical:

- “Next time, place the camera 2-3m away at chest height, 30-45 degrees from the front.”
- “This angle helps judge whether the elbow path and torso stay stable.”
